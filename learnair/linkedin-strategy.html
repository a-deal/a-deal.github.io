LinkedIn Content Strategy

Why LinkedIn, what to post, how to think about cadence. Start with one post.

March 25, 2026

Why LinkedIn, Why Now

LinkedIn is the only platform where Mike’s target audience already lives: veteran hiring managers, workforce development professionals, defense contractors, HR leaders, L&D directors, and the LearnAIR team. Justin, Teresa, Bobby, Trisha, Joe. They’re all there. Every post Mike writes, they see.

LinkedIn rewards three things that play to Mike’s strengths:

01
Authenticity over polish
Real experience beats produced content. Mike’s journey from “what is a terminal?” to building with AI in 3 weeks is the content.
02
Professional context
Unlike X where you’re shouting into the void, LinkedIn posts land in the feeds of people who hire, fund, and partner. The audience is already filtered.
03
Saves are the king metric
When someone saves your post, LinkedIn’s algorithm treats it as the highest signal of value. Posts that teach something specific get saved. Mike’s mission command insights are inherently saveable.
Why it matters for LearnAIR

If Mike is posting about mission command and AI supervision before the 4-way call, Justin and Teresa see someone who isn’t just talking in meetings but building in public. That’s a different level of commitment.

  • Creates demand signal — if veterans and employers react, that’s proof the thesis resonates
  • Builds Mike’s personal brand independent of LearnAIR — that’s leverage in any negotiation
  • Generates testimonial content in real time — his “oh shit” moments become proof points
  • Attracts the exact people we need: employers feeling the AI gap, veterans looking for direction, workforce professionals who get it

Starter Posts

Pick one. Don’t overthink it. The first post is the hardest. After that, it gets easier.

Post 1 The Journey
Three weeks ago I didn’t know what a terminal was. Not a flight terminal. A computer terminal. I’d been using ChatGPT like everyone else, typing questions and getting answers. Then my brother showed me something different. He opened a black screen, typed a sentence, and an AI agent read our entire project, wrote a research brief, and published it to a website. In one prompt. No clicking, no menus, no copy-paste. I’m a Marine infantry officer turned management consultant turned breathwork facilitator. Tech was never my lane. But watching what’s possible now, I realized the gap isn’t technical skill. It’s knowing how to manage these systems. And that’s something I’ve been trained to do my entire career. In the military we call it mission command. Define the boundaries, set the zone of autonomy, validate the outcomes. That’s exactly what AI needs right now. Not more engineers. More supervisors. I’m early in this journey. But the through-line is already clear. #AI #veterans #missioncommand #workforcedevelopment #AIliteracy
Post 2 The Thesis
AI has given every individual 10x leverage. The question nobody’s answered: how do you capture that at the organizational level? The bottleneck isn’t the technology. It’s the processes, coordination, and accountability that haven’t caught up. Veterans spend their careers managing exactly this kind of problem. In the military it’s called mission command. Defining boundaries, validating outcomes, driving results through ambiguity. That skill set transfers directly to AI ecosystem supervision. And almost nobody is talking about it yet. Working on something here. More to come. #AI #veterans #workforcetransformation
Post 3 The Learning Moment
Just finished a session on Custom GPTs and AI agents. One person in the room built a fully functional HR compliance portal in under an hour. From scratch. That’s the individual productivity story. And it’s real. But here’s what I keep coming back to: what happens when 50 people in your company each build their own tool? Who validates the outputs? Who coordinates the overlap? Who’s accountable when the AI-generated policy template gets the FLSA requirements wrong? That’s the gap. Individual building is solved. Organizational management of all that building is wide open. Curious: anyone else seeing this in their org? #AI #workforcedesign #leadership

Cadence

Don’t batch-write. Write from what happened that day or that week. Freshness over perfection.

Week 1
One post
Just one. See what happens. Don’t overthink it.
Weeks 2–4
2 posts / week
One about learning, one about the thesis.
Month 2+
3 posts / week
If the energy is there. Quality over quantity, always.

Commenting Strategy

More important than posting at first. Comments on other people’s posts build visibility faster than your own posts.

Targets

  • Teresa Coats — posts on LinkedIn about AI workforce
  • LearnAIR company posts
  • Erik Brynjolfsson — 487 posts, very active
  • Gina Raimondo — posts about workforce policy
  • VetsinTech, Code Platoon, SkillBridge-related content
Rule: Add substance, not “great post!” One sentence of real insight or a question. That’s it.

What to Track

Don’t chase likes. Chase saves and comments. One thoughtful comment from an employer or veteran is worth more than 100 likes.

Impressions
Are people seeing it?
Saves
Are people bookmarking it? Highest signal of value.
Comments
Are people engaging? What are they saying?
Profile Views
Are new people finding Mike?
Connection Requests
Are the right people reaching out?

Format Tips

Quick Reference
  • Short paragraphs. One thought per paragraph. Mobile-first.
  • Hook in the first line. LinkedIn truncates after ~2 lines. The first sentence has to earn the click.
  • No hashtag spam. 3–5 relevant hashtags max, at the bottom.
  • End with a question. Drives comments, which drive reach.
  • Voice: Grounded. First-person. “Here’s what I’m learning” not “here’s what you should do.”
  • Links: Add the one-pager when the time is right (not in the first post). Never link to internal hub or strategy docs.
The meta point: Mike posting on LinkedIn is curriculum in action. He’s learning to communicate the thesis publicly. That’s a skill he’ll need when talking to employers, funders, and veterans. The posts are practice reps for the real conversations. And if they resonate, they become the demand signal that validates everything.
