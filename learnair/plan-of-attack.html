Steelman Against Our Approach

01
Research project, not a business
20+ pages of analysis. Zero revenue. Zero veterans trained. Zero employers engaged. Research is the most seductive form of procrastination for smart people. The test: if you deleted every document and had to explain this to an employer in 60 seconds, could you?
02
Mission command → AI supervision is unvalidated
The metaphor is compelling but nobody has tested whether a veteran with mission command experience actually manages AI systems better than a non-veteran PM with 3 years of experience. Managing a language model is not like managing a patrol. Feedback loops, judgment cadence, ambiguity type are all different. Need one veteran, one employer, one real task to test it.
03
Optimizing for the wrong audience
Most energy spent pitching LearnAIR (Teresa, Justin, Trisha). They’re a potential partner, not a customer. Zero conversations with actual employers or veterans. Partnership will sort itself once there’s demand signal. Demand comes from customers.
04
Consultancy play is a different business
Consulting = time-for-money. Training program = curriculum-for-outcomes. Different economics, different sales, different value props. If you’re good consultants, you’ll be too busy to build the program. Pick one identity. The other supports it.
05
Mike is 3 weeks into learning AI
Installed Claude Code March 20. Asked “what is a hammer?” on Session 2. Enthusiastic and learning fast, but not ready to train anyone. Needs 2–3 months of daily use for credibility. Andrew delivers technical side, Mike delivers veteran/leadership side. That’s the realistic split.
06
Oregon might be the wrong market
47th in business climate. No active-duty bases. <1% GDP from DoD. 10–12K veterans in Deschutes County. How many are in prime cohort, available, interested? Maybe 200. Compare to San Antonio, NoVA, San Diego: 10–100x density. Remote-first with in-person intensives might be the right model.
07
Moving fast ≠ moving forward
Thesis opener: 4+ iterations. Hub: redesigned 3 times. Concept section: rewritten 5 times. Extraordinary output. A lot of lateral movement. Polishing can substitute for shipping. The one thing that moves the needle more than anything: a phone call to an employer.
