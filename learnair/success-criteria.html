What Does Success Look Like Tuesday?

Three frameworks applied to the April 7 call. K asks if we're converging. Duke asks if we've pre-decided signal. Taleb asks who bears the downside.

April 3, 2026

The purpose of Tuesday: make them bet or fold. Either answer moves us forward. Success is a shared commitment with skin, or a clean no. The only failure is ambiguity.

Komoroske Are we default-converging?

Right now the LearnAIR relationship is default-diverging. We have a thesis, a framework, a competitive landscape. Justin and Teresa have a brand, SDVOSB status, clients, and a waitlist page. These exist in parallel. They don't cohere yet.

Success = the call moves the relationship from "interesting parallel work" to "shared OODA loop."

  • We leave with a shared understanding of what we're building together (even if rough)
  • There's a concrete next action that requires both sides to show up
  • The next conversation is about executing, not re-pitching

Failure = we leave with enthusiasm but no shared loop. "Love it, let's keep talking" without a date, a deliverable, or a commitment from their side. That's still diverging. Energy without coherence.

K's test: Can you describe the next two weeks in one sentence that includes both sides doing something? If yes, the call worked. If it's just "we'll keep building and circle back," it didn't.

Annie Duke Pre-decide what "signal" means

We're about to enter an emotionally charged 30 minutes where confirmation bias will run hot. Mike wants it to work (3 months runway). Andrew wants it to work (invested 3 weeks). Justin and Teresa want to feel excited. Everyone has incentives to call it a win regardless of what actually happens.

Pre-decide the signal levels before Tuesday:

OutcomeWhat it meansNext move
They offer a follow-up date + content access Real signal. They're investing time. Schedule working session, begin curriculum adaptation
They say "love it" but deflect all three asks Polite pass. Enthusiasm is not commitment. Wait 5 days. If no follow-up initiated by them, the answer is no. Don't chase.
They propose merging our work into existing curriculum Absorption risk. They want our IP without a separate track. Hold the line: "The supervision program needs its own track." If they resist, that's the answer.
They raise Texas, timeline uncertainty, "figuring things out" Drift signal. Not ready to commit. "What would need to be true for you to commit to a pilot by June?"

Backcasting test

Imagine it's April 14. What artifact or commitment from Tuesday makes you say "that call was worth it"? Write that down before the call. That's the success criteria.

10-10-10

  • 10 minutes after: You'll feel good regardless (adrenaline, rapport). Don't judge the call here.
  • 10 days after: Did anything actually change? Did they follow through? This is where real signal lives.
  • 10 months from now: Was this where the partnership formed, or where you learned it wasn't going to?

All three outcomes are fine. The worst outcome is ambiguity that drags for weeks.

Taleb Who bears the downside?

Skin in the game check. Who loses something if this doesn't work?

Tuesday's purpose is to get them to put something on the table. Not money. Time. Content access. An employer introduction. A follow-up with a deliverable. Anything that costs them something if they don't follow through.

The barbell strategy

Don't bet everything on the LearnAIR path. The BreakLine/BetterUp angle from the hiring analysis is the other tail. If Tuesday goes well, LearnAIR is the primary path. If it doesn't, we already have the competitor hiring map and the trojan horse play. Antifragile positioning: we benefit from Tuesday going well AND we benefit from learning it's not the right path.

Via negativa

Stop trying to prove the thesis is right on Tuesday. Instead, try to find out if it's wrong. If we can't kill it in 30 minutes with the people who should be our partners, it's probably real. The strongest thing Mike can say: "We came to test this with you, not to sell it to you. Where does this break?"

Turkey problem check

Have we been feeding ourselves a narrative for 3 weeks that feels increasingly inevitable? Every day we work on this, the sunk cost makes it harder to walk away. Tuesday is the circuit breaker. The longer you go without a disconfirming event, the more fragile you are to one.

The Answer

Success is not "they like us." Success is one of two things:

1. A shared commitment with skin. A follow-up date on the calendar, content access granted, and at least one employer introduction from their network. Three small bets from their side. Costs them maybe 3 hours total. If they won't put in 3 hours, they won't put in 3 months.

2. A clean no. They're not ready, not interested, or not aligned. That's also success because it frees us to take the thesis directly to BreakLine, BetterUp, or employers. A clean no in April is worth more than a maybe that drags to June.

The one thing that is NOT success: a warm, excited conversation that produces no commitment and no rejection. That's the ambiguity trap. Duke calls it "resulting from politeness." K calls it "default diverging with good vibes." Taleb calls it "the turkey's good day."

How This Connects to the Story Arc

The arc is the what we say. Five beats, 10 minutes, spoken delivery. This doc is the what we're listening for. Signal levels, kill criteria, skin-in-the-game audit.

They connect at two specific points:

1. Beat 5 is where this doc lives or dies

The arc builds momentum through Beats 1-4, then Beat 5 makes three asks: employer signal, curriculum access, follow-up with a date. This doc pre-decides what each response to those asks actually means. Without it, we deliver Beat 5 and freestyle the interpretation afterward when confirmation bias is hottest. With it, we already know: content access + follow-up date = real signal. "Love it, let's keep talking" with no commitment = polite pass, wait 5 days.

2. Via negativa reframes the arc's posture

The arc is currently structured as a pitch. Taleb's input suggests a subtle shift. Instead of proving we're right, we invite them to break it. Mike's one-liner stays the same. The beats stay the same. But the energy shifts from "we've built something amazing, here it is" to "we've built something we believe in, help us stress-test it." That's more honest given where we are (zero employer signal), and it gives Justin and Teresa a role in the conversation other than audience.
