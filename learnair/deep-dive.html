Competitive Position

Strengths

  • SDVOSB opens government contract doors (5% federal set-aside)
  • Bobby Napiltonia brings enterprise credibility + Rolodex
  • OpenAI partnership is real
  • SHRM credits enable HR/L&D department sales
  • Bend, Oregon base — perception advantage for non-coastal tech
  • Justin’s exit history (Amazon agency) = knows how to build and sell

Weaknesses

  • Bootstrapped, small, family-heavy team
  • No public pricing, no third-party reviews
  • Limited content output (2 blog posts)
  • ChatGPT-only = platform risk
  • “1 million veterans” is aspirational with zero evidence of traction
  • Veteran program is a waitlist, not a product
Key question: Is LearnAIR a training company that wants to add a veteran program, or a veteran-focused company? Right now it’s clearly the former — the veteran initiative is marketing, not a business unit.

Easter Seals Oregon — Funding Context

  • HVRP: $1.4M federal (DOL), ~$71K other. Career exploration, skills assessment, job readiness, vocational counseling, computer training, financial literacy, 90/180/270-day retention follow-up.
  • SSVF: Housing assistance, employment support, benefits navigation.
  • Service locations: Portland, McMinnville, Salem, Roseburg, Eugene, Grants Pass, Klamath Falls, Medford.
  • Model: Federal funding → Easter Seals → contracts with specialized training providers. This is the standard pathway for LearnAIR to deliver AI training to veterans via DOL funding.
