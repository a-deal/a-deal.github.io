Concept Brief

Mission Command → AI Supervision

A concept for the LearnAIR veteran program

The Gap

The hardest problem in AI isn't building it. It's managing what happens when an entire workforce becomes 10x more productive and the processes, coordination, and accountability structures haven't caught up. That's a redesign challenge playing out across every sector.

This role is emerging in real time. It sits between the AI system and the business outcome. Defining where AI operates, validating what it produces, keeping humans accountable for results. Every industry is working to define it.

The Concept

The Opportunity

An applied experiential training program that transitions veterans with mission command skill sets into AI ecosystem supervision roles. Built at the pace this moment demands.

01 — The Veteran

Veterans possess the foundational skill set to command AI. In the military, it’s called mission command: defining boundaries, setting the zone of autonomy, validating outcomes, driving results through ambiguity.

02 — The Training Program

Applied experiential curriculum that bridges mission command to AI ecosystem supervision. Designed to inform and adapt LearnAIR’s proven training methodology for the veteran population.

03 — The Employer

Every industry deploying AI at scale needs people who can manage what AI produces and own the outcome.

04 — The Funder

DOL grants, GI Bill, SkillBridge, employer training budgets. Veterans train at no cost.

Why Now

  • AI task capability doubles every 4–7 months. The window to train supervisors is closing fast. METR / Stanford AI Index 2025
  • 13% employment decline for ages 22–25 in AI-exposed jobs since ChatGPT launch. Junior roles are disappearing first. Brynjolfsson et al., “Canaries in the Coal Mine,” Stanford 2025
  • “America cannot withstand the economic shock that’s coming.” Former Commerce Secretary Raimondo calls for outcomes-based, employer-driven training. NYT Op-Ed, Mar 2026

The gap between “AI literacy” and “AI ecosystem supervision” is where veterans have a natural, immediate advantage.

Who We Are

Mike Deal, the veteran perspective. Marine Corps infantry officer (2004–2012). Two deployments to Fallujah, Iraq. Post-military: Deloitte management consulting (DOD, global joint ventures), EFT practitioner, Holotropic Breathwork® facilitator, Forest Service ranger. Based in Bend, Oregon.

Andrew Deal, the technical perspective. Startup operator. Two exits (Bear Flag Robotics → John Deere, Over → GoDaddy). Identity systems at Twitch (10M+ DAU). $39M in autonomous operations funding. Product and technical leadership across ag-tech, construction tech, mining tech, and defense tech. Oxford, Reforge (SV’s premier product leadership program). Upskilled into engineering through a boot camp, not academia. Firsthand experience in rapid skill transitions. Based in San Francisco.

Mike reached out to Andrew to learn AI. In the process, they started building the exact thing this program needs: a training approach, a curriculum, and a way to think about how veterans fit into this AI workforce moment. The collaboration is anchored in the merger of their backgrounds and experience.

What We’ve Built

Research and frameworks to inform how LearnAIR’s established AI training methodology could be adapted for the veteran population.

  • Competitive landscape analysis (10+ programs mapped).
  • 4-week pilot curriculum design with validate-or-kill criteria.
  • Policy alignment research (Raimondo, Brynjolfsson, Stanford HAI).
  • Oregon employer and funding landscape (concrete organizations, institutions, grant pathways).
  • Field manual: actively training ourselves through the curriculum we’re designing. We’re the first cohort.
  • Curriculum stubs in development: persona frameworks, glossary of AI ecosystem supervision terms, applied experiential learning methodology.
