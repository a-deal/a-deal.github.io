Concept Brief

Mission Command → AI Supervision

A concept for the LearnAIR veteran program

The Gap

Deloitte teaches prompts. Code Platoon teaches coding. SkillBridge teaches existing job skills. Nobody trains the person who sits between the AI system and the business outcome—the one who makes sure it actually works.

That’s what mission command trained veterans for: managing complex systems under uncertainty, making judgment calls with incomplete information, and owning outcomes.

The AI operations supervisor is the most important role nobody is hiring for yet.

The Concept

The Student

Veterans at any transition stage. Military judgment—leading teams, running ops under pressure, managing systems—translates directly to AI supervision.

The Employer

Defense contractors, federal agencies, and SMBs modernizing with AI. They need people who can manage AI operations, not just use AI tools.

The Funder

DOL grants (via Easter Seals HVRP), GI Bill, SkillBridge, employer training budgets. Veterans train at no cost to them.

Why Now

  • AI task capability doubles every 4–7 months—the window to train supervisors is closing fast. METR / Stanford AI Index 2025
  • 13% employment decline for ages 22–25 in AI-exposed jobs since ChatGPT launch. Junior roles are disappearing first. Brynjolfsson et al., “Canaries in the Coal Mine,” Stanford 2025
  • “America cannot withstand the economic shock that’s coming”—former Commerce Secretary Raimondo calls for outcomes-based, employer-driven training. NYT Op-Ed, Mar 2026
  • The gap between “AI literacy” and “AI operations supervision” is where veterans have a natural, immediate advantage.

What We’ve Built

  • Competitive landscape analysis (10+ programs mapped)
  • 4-week pilot curriculum with validate-or-kill criteria
  • Policy alignment research (Raimondo, Brynjolfsson, Stanford HAI)
  • Demand validation framework with employer outreach scripts
  • AI onboarding field manual: actively training ourselves through the curriculum we're designing. Crawl/walk/run learning path, tool selection framework, personalization methods. We're the first cohort.
