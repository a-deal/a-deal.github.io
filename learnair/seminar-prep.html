Why This Matters

Sarkar’s research IS your thesis stated in academic language. His key finding: “Agents shift worker effort from implementation to supervision, which especially benefits verifiable work and expert workers.” That is literally “AI operations supervisor” described by a UChicago professor with data from 1,000 organizations. His numbers: 39% increase in code output, experienced workers 6% higher agent-accept rates per standard deviation of experience, quality unchanged (no increase in reverts or bugs).

This is not a tangential connection. He proved the role exists. You’re building the training program to fill it.

Brynjolfsson hosts these weekly. He’s the person to build a relationship with. His “Canaries” paper (13% employment decline for 22–25 year olds in AI-exposed jobs) and the augmentation vs automation distinction are the research backbone of our thesis.

Q&A Questions (Pick One)

Have several ready. The obvious ones will get asked first. Your question is your introduction before the hallway pitch. Ask one, not two.

01 The supervision bottleneck (safest, pure research): “You show agents shift effort from implementation to supervision. As agent capability improves, do you see supervision itself becoming the bottleneck, or does the ratio of supervision-to-output stay constant?” 02 The experience gap mechanism (shows you read the paper): “Experienced workers accept agent outputs at higher rates. Is that because they’re better at prompting, or better at evaluating outputs? Those seem like different skills with different training implications.” 03 Organizational design (bridges to your thesis): “Your findings suggest verifiable work environments benefit most from agents. Does that imply organizations need to redesign work to be more verifiable before they can capture agent productivity gains?” 04 Workforce readiness (closer to your thesis, still academic): “If the key skill is delegation and evaluation rather than implementation, what does that imply for workforce development? Are we training people for the wrong layer of the stack?” 05 Structured-execution populations (only if the room has gone there): “The delegation and evaluation skills you’re describing map closely to military command frameworks, where leaders set intent and evaluate execution without doing the work themselves. Have you looked at populations with that kind of structured decision-making background?”

Strategy

Start with questions 1–3. Only go to 4–5 if the conversation has already moved toward workforce or training implications. Questions 1–3 are pure research questions that make you memorable. Questions 4–5 connect to your thesis but risk sounding like a pitch in academic clothing. Read the room.
