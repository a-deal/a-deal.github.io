What LearnAIR Actually Is

PAUL'S CLARITY TEST

One sentence: LearnAIR is a Bend, Oregon AI training company that teaches businesses how to use ChatGPT — and they want to build a veteran-specific program but haven’t started yet.

The veteran program opportunity in plain English

  1. Veterans need AI skills to be competitive in the job market
  2. Employers need people who can manage AI systems (not just use them)
  3. Government has workforce development money (DOL, VA, SkillBridge) looking for programs to fund
  4. LearnAIR has the training content + OpenAI partnership + SDVOSB status to access that funding
  5. Nobody has connected these dots into an actual program yet — it’s a waitlist page

Who pays

Not the veteran. Three funding sources:

  • Government grants (DOL workforce programs via orgs like Easter Seals)
  • Employer-sponsored (companies that want AI-ready veteran hires)
  • GI Bill / SkillBridge pipeline (if accredited)

What Mike + Andrew bring that LearnAIR doesn’t have

  • A specific thesis: mission command → AI operations supervision (not just “AI literacy”)
  • A concrete curriculum design (4-week pilot with validate-or-kill criteria)
  • Outcomes-based framing (placement, not attendance)
  • Andrew’s technical depth (building with AI, not just prompting)
  • Mike’s veteran credibility + in-person partnership skills
  • Connection to policy conversation (Stanford/Hoover, Raimondo, Singapore model)

Questions for Justin

Understanding Intent

  1. When you say “building a team” — what does that mean? Contractors? Partners? Employees?
  2. What’s the veteran program’s current state beyond the waitlist page? Any pilots run? Any vets trained through it?
  3. Is there funding committed, or is this “build it first, find money second”?
  4. What’s the Trisha/Easter Seals connection specifically — active contract or exploratory?

Testing Alignment

  1. “Train 1 million veterans” — what’s the actual Year 1 target?
  2. Is the veteran program a separate business unit or a marketing arm of the corporate training?
  3. How do you measure success — training completions or job placements?
  4. What’s the engagement model for vets — free, subsidized, employer-sponsored?

Strategic

  1. OpenAI-only or open to model-agnostic? (Claude, Gemini, open-source)
  2. Bobby’s involvement — day-to-day or advisory?
  3. What’s the decision timeline? When does this team need to be moving?
  4. How do you think about the SkillBridge pipeline?

Things Mike Should NOT Do on This Call

  • Don’t pitch the full briefing yet — listen first, understand Justin’s vision
  • Don’t commit to any structure (contractor, partner, etc.) — say “I want to think on that”
  • Don’t mention Andrew’s involvement yet unless it comes up naturally — keep that card for later
  • Don’t oversell the thesis — ask questions, let Justin feel heard
  • DO take notes on: what Justin says the obstacles are, what he’s tried, what he wishes he had

What to Listen For

Green Lights
  • Justin talks about outcomes, not just training hours
  • There’s actual funding or a clear path to it
  • He’s open to co-ownership / equity, not just contractor work
  • He acknowledges the program needs building, not just marketing
  • Bobby is actually involved and accessible
Red Flags
  • “We just need someone to deliver the existing curriculum to veterans” (you’re labor, not a partner)
  • No funding, no timeline, no specifics (it’s a dream, not a plan)
  • “We’ve already designed the program” (no room for the thesis)
  • Heavy focus on “1 million” number (vanity metric, no path to get there)
  • Trisha’s Easter Seals contract is vague or nonexistent

Post-Call Actions

  1. Mike sends Andrew the full transcript (Notion, Gemini, or manual recording)
  2. Andrew processes transcript, extracts key signals
  3. Evening session: assess fit, decide whether to go deeper or pivot to independent play
  4. If green: iterate on the briefing with Justin’s actual language and constraints
  5. If red: focus energy on building independently using the thesis + curriculum as standalone assets
