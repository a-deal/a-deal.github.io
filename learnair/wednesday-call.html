Kill criteria

🟢 Green — Picks in the room + names a date for written confirmation Friday proposal goes out. Yes-or-no required by April 22.
🟡 Yellow — Picks but defers commit 7 days. April 22 deadline. After that, the answer is no.
🔴 Red — Can't pick / asks for "more time on framing" That IS the answer. Walk warm. No Friday proposal.

Things to protect

  • No single hourly rate named — the options ARE the negotiation.
  • The 3 returned questions (starting point / first hurdle / ideal scenario) → punt to Friday proposal.
  • Don't agree to a 4th meeting in the room.
  • Don't custom-build Option D live with them.
  • Phases 1–2 are Mike's. Andrew stays quiet.
  • Don't name a single Andrew+Mike rate. The number lives inside the option, not as a separate line.

Mike risks to watch

  1. Softens questions to make them feel better. If he qualifies Option B with "maybe" or "if you're up for it," he dilutes it. Don't correct him in the room — debrief tonight.
  2. Accepts "we'll think about it" without a date. Andrew steps in: "What date works for you to come back with a pick?"
  3. Reads vibes over numbers. If his post-call read is 80% energy and 20% commitments, run the 8-item checklist on him before he editorializes.

After the call

  • Don't editorialize with Mike on the way out.
  • Tonight: 8-item debrief checklist before he tells you how it felt.
  • If green: Thursday morning, draft the Friday proposal. Mike writes first, Andrew edits.
  • If yellow: hold for 4/22. No Thursday action.
  • If red: warm close. Run Mochary on Mike that night with "What did today tell you about what this is actually going to be?"
