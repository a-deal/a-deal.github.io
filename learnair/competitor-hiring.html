That's the gap we fill.

Strategic Implications

The pattern: Veteran orgs are scaling distribution (partner managers, program directors, fundraisers) but not AI capability. Nobody is hiring AI curriculum designers or AI operations trainers. That role doesn't exist yet in these organizations.

Two ways to use this intelligence

1. Trojan horse. Mike takes a role at BreakLine or HOH, builds relationships from inside, and surfaces the AI supervision gap with employers directly. He'd have access to 200+ employer partners (BreakLine) or the U.S. Chamber network (HOH). This gives us demand-side signal without cold outreach.

2. Partnership pitch. Use this hiring data on the April 7 call. "BreakLine is hiring partner managers, not AI trainers. Code Platoon is hiring fundraisers, not AI curriculum designers. That's the gap we fill." This is concrete market evidence, not just our thesis.

BetterUp as a strategic angle

BetterUp has government clients (Air Force, NASA, FAA), ran a veteran coaching pilot (73 service members, 6 months), and their Senior UX Researcher role is embedded in the AI team. Alex Ha (Mike's former manager) was VP there. A role at BetterUp would give direct access to the employer side of veteran AI adoption, plus government procurement relationships.
