Phase 1 — Warm Open

~3 minutes · No screen share yet · Just you, Justin, and Teresa

You open. Reference the gap since the last call, ask about the move, set the tone. Don't rush into business. The first 90 seconds set the temperature of the whole call.

Justin, Teresa — really glad we're back at it. Feels like we've been doing this dance over email for a couple weeks now and I'm happy we're finally face to face. How's the move-prep going? You guys getting closer to a date?
Brief listening pause. Let them share. Match their energy. Don't immediately pivot.

When the small talk lands and the room feels open, set the frame for the call:

Andrew and I have spent a lot of time the last few days really working through what this could look like, and we're walking in today with conviction about the shape of the partnership and how we want to spend this hour with you. So if you're up for it, here's what I'd love to do: I'm going to walk you through what we've been building — the thesis, the foundation, the columns, how it sequences — and then Andrew is going to come in and take you through the structure side, including the question we left hanging from Monday. Sound good?
Pause for affirmation. When they say yes, share your screen and pull up the v6-slim proposal.

Phase 2 — Walk the Slim Proposal

~12 minutes · Share screen on the v6-slim proposal · Eight sections

You're walking Justin and Teresa through the slim proposal in order, top to bottom. Don't read it word-for-word — that's death. You point, you summarize, you connect, you let the page do the visual work while you do the human work. Eight sections, ~90 seconds each.

1. The Header

Point at: the title and the "April 2026 · 90-day working agreement" stamp.
Okay so this is the v6-slim proposal Andrew and I put together. It's the 90-day working agreement we want to walk you through. Not a contract — a working architecture for how Andrew and I would come in alongside LearnAIR for the next 90 days. The deeper version exists; this is the version we want you to feel.

2. The Thesis

Point at: the gold pull-quote in italics on the dark background.

This is the thesis. "You're not training people to use AI. You're training leaders to command AI-enabled systems." That's the line. That's the lens we want to put on everything LearnAIR is already doing.

Nobody is training the person who sits between the AI system and the business outcome and makes sure it actually works. That's the supervision gap. That's what military planning trained veterans for. And that's the role employers are hiring for right now.

3. The Market Anchor

Point at: the big gold "$168K–$231K" stat.
$168 to $231 thousand plus equity. That's what Ramp is hiring "Agentic Operators" at right now. Justin, you sent us the posting hours after our first call — you saw it before we did. That's the role. That's the market. It's not abstract. The job exists, the salary band is real, and right now nobody is training for it on purpose.

4. The Reframe — "Foundation before frame"

Point at: the "Foundation before frame" headline and the body paragraph below it.

Here's the reframe for what these 90 days actually are. Foundation before frame. Before you frame walls or set a roof, you agree on the foundation. The 90 days isn't us trying to ship a finished program. It's us proving the foundation is sound.

The desired end state at day 90 is: LearnAIR and the Andrew + Mike unit have approved the architectural foundation and framing of the Veterans + AI initiative, and we're ready to continue under a longer-term contracting relationship. That's the bar. Not "did we ship a course." It's "did we prove the foundation."

5. Three Foundational Columns

Point at: walk all three column cards in order — 01 Veteran Outreach → 02 Partnership Outreach → 03 Agentic Curriculum Development. Don't dwell, but name what each one IS.

This is the meat of the 90 days — three foundational columns that the engagement delivers against.

Column one: Veteran Outreach. Recruiting and engaging veterans across existing networks and TAPS pathways. Building the audience the curriculum serves. This is where a lot of the relationship work happens — me leaning on networks and the veteran-services ecosystem.

Column two: Partnership Outreach. Building the employer pipeline and the network relationships that validate what the curriculum is teaching. Every employer conversation is evidence for the 90-day review. This is also work I drive — the human element, the relationships, the framing.

Column three: Agentic Curriculum Development. Designing and beginning to evolve the Evergreen curriculum. Topical, model-agnostic, architected so it doesn't expire when the next model drops. This is where Andrew leans in heavy — curriculum architecture is his lane.

Three columns. They run in parallel, they reinforce each other. That's the foundation.

6. Three Phases — sequential or parallel

Point at: the "Three phases, sequential or parallel" headline and walk Phases 1 → 2 → 3 below it.

This is how the work sequences. Three phases — they can run sequentially, they can run in parallel depending on what we have to put against it.

Phase 1: Create the veteran foothold. Connect the existing LearnAIR curriculum to veterans. Recruit through networks. Identify funding pathways. Begin the military overlay.

Phase 2: Build the vet outreach strategy. Develop a 2-to-3 hour engagement hook. Deploy across the veteran spectrum. Begin evolving the curriculum toward agentic content.

Phase 3: Agentic architecture. Build the agentic curriculum framework. Deploy to the broader LearnAIR community. Establish building blocks for the longer-term program.

The reason phases instead of one big plan is so we have honest checkpoints. Each phase produces evidence the next phase actually fits.

7. Evergreen by Design

Point at: the "Evergreen by design" headline and read the two paragraphs in your own voice.

Here's the curriculum philosophy that holds the whole thing together. Evergreen by design. Topical, not tool-based. Model-agnostic on purpose. Whether someone uses ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini is below the line. What we teach is above the line — the judgment layer that makes veterans effective supervisors of AI-enabled systems.

The tools change. The judgment doesn't. That means LearnAIR can run cohort after cohort without rebuilding the curriculum from scratch every time the next model drops. And it means this scaffold can be repurposed for non-veteran audiences too — the same judgment layer applies.

8. What This Unlocks for LearnAIR

Point at: the "A veteran-first wedge and a reusable scaffold" headline, then walk the three unlock bullets in order.

And this is what I want to land on before I hand it to Andrew. What this actually unlocks for LearnAIR specifically.

One — a clear path into TAPS and the veteran-services ecosystem. A dedicated delivery vehicle for the 2-to-3 hour Track 1 hook, with access pathways through existing network relationships. That's a wedge LearnAIR doesn't have today.

Two — an employer pipeline that validates the curriculum. Every employer conversation informs curriculum design and creates placement signal. That's the work I drive directly. It's how we know the curriculum is teaching what employers actually need.

Three — an evergreen curriculum that leverages what LearnAIR already teaches. Not a parallel program. A military overlay on the existing AI fluency curriculum, architected so it can scale beyond veterans to LearnAIR's broader student base.

So this isn't us asking you to build something new. It's us proposing a wedge that uses what you already have, sharpens it for veterans, and gives you a reusable scaffold for everything that comes after.
