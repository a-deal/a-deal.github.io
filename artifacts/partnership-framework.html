Where we agree

The big stuff is settled

LLC is the right entity.

No argument. Bootstrapped, zebra economics, pass-through taxation. C-Corp would be over-engineering for what we're building.

Vesting protects both of us.

Completely agree. Neither of us should be locked in without an earn-in period. Clean off-ramps are a feature, not a bug.

IP assignment to the LLC.

Yes. All existing and future IP (Kiso, Kasane iOS, brand, domain, everything) belongs to the entity. This is foundational.

Tax distributions, dispute resolution, non-compete.

All good as proposed. Mediation first, narrow non-compete, required K-1 distributions. These are clean and fair.

We're building a zebra.

Not optimizing for exit. Building a sustainable business that pays us well and does meaningful work. Fully aligned.

Where I see it differently

The frame

Your proposal frames the partnership as "founder bringing in a partner." I've been sitting with whether the frame might be closer to "two people building something together that neither could build alone." Curious how you see it. Let me share where I'm coming from.

The contribution picture

Paul's prior work

2+ years of product thinking, the Kasane brand, the 130-person waitlist, the Stanford clinical network, the behavior change methodology, and the iOS app. That foundation matters, and I'm not dismissing it.

Andrew's contribution

Kiso: 605 tests, 40+ MCP tools, multi-user data isolation, OAuth, structured logging, deterministic scheduler, clinical-grade scoring. Milo: real coaching agent serving users today. Wearable integrations (Garmin, Oura, Whoop, Apple Health). The shared data layer that makes everything connect.

I think the honest assessment is that we've both made significant contributions, in different domains. Neither piece works as well without the other, and that's actually the strongest argument for a structure that reflects true partnership.

Capital interest, not profits interest

The profits interest structure is tax-efficient and well-reasoned. I understand why you proposed it. My concern is more about what it signals than what it does mechanically.

Capital interest means you're an owner. Profits interest means you participate in upside. In practice, the day-to-day difference is small. In a dissolution or sale, the capital interest holder gets paid first. I'm wondering whether two classes of member in a two-person partnership might work against the alignment we both want. How are you thinking about that?

I'd lean toward both of us holding capital interest, with the prior-work premium showing up in the split rather than the type of interest. What's your reaction to that?

The split

Your proposal

70/30

→

My proposal

55/45

55 recognizes that you started earlier, built the brand, developed the methodology, and have relationships that matter. 45 recognizes that I've built the production infrastructure, the intelligence layer, and the coaching system that makes this a defensible product rather than another habit app.

This isn't about counting lines of code against years of prior work. It's about setting up a structure where both of us feel fully invested for the next 3-5 years. I think 55/45 does that. I think 70/30 could create friction if the day-to-day contributions end up being roughly equal going forward, which is how I'm seeing the trajectory.

I'm genuinely open to discussing this. Is there a way you're valuing the prior contributions that I'm not seeing? I want to make sure I'm weighing this fully.

Mutual vesting

Your proposal has only me vesting. I think we should both vest.

If we're both committed for the long haul (and I believe we are), mutual vesting costs neither of us anything. It signals that we're both earning our stake through continued contribution, not just through what came before. And it protects both of us symmetrically: if I leave early, you're protected. If you leave early, I'm protected.

Proposed schedule: 3-year vest, 6-month cliff, monthly vesting after cliff. Applies to both members equally.

Co-managing members

Your proposal designates you as sole Managing Member. I understand the reasoning: you have the deepest context on the product vision, clinical network, and business relationships.

I'd propose co-managing members with domain-specific authority:

Paul's domain

Product design, UX, iOS development, behavior change methodology, clinical relationships, App Store strategy

Andrew's domain

Technical architecture, infrastructure, data layer, AI/ML, deployment, backend API design

Business decisions (pricing, strategy, spending above $500/mo, new hires, partnerships, pivots) require mutual consent.

This isn't about distrust. It's about reflecting how we actually work. You don't need my permission to redesign the Today screen. I don't need yours to restructure the database. We both need each other's input on whether to raise prices or bring on a third person.
