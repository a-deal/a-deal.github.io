Architecture

Three layers, built sequentially

LAYER 1: COLLECTION
See what you've tried

"Your deck" shows which of the 20 habits you've adopted. Start at 0/20. Each time you work with Milo on a new habit, it appears in your deck. Visual satisfaction of building a collection. No new content needed, just a view on what already exists.

LAYER 2: PROGRESSION
Level up through real outcomes

Each habit has tiers: bronze (doing it), silver (doing it well), gold (mastery). Tiers are earned through streaks AND measured outcomes from wearable data. "Consistent bedtime" at gold means your Oura shows 7.5hrs with sleep score above 80, not just that you checked a box. This is where biometrics connect to habits.

LAYER 3: EXPANSION (FUTURE)
Grow the catalogue

50+ habits over time. Goal-specific packs. Community contributions with citation requirements. This is the "250" in "40/250." Not needed for v1. The 20 we have are the right 20 for the ICP we're targeting.

Integration

Where each layer lives

KASANE (APP)

Collection view, tier badges, visual progression. This is where the magic is felt. The deck metaphor is inherently visual.

KISO (BACKEND)

Tier calculation from wearable data + streak data. Unlock logic. The backend already has streak tracking and wearable_daily metrics.

MILO (COACHING)

Celebrates tier-ups. Suggests the next habit to collect based on goals and readiness. "You've nailed sleep consistency. Ready to add protein timing to your deck?"

THE CONVERGENCE TEST

A collectible system with progression tiers needs content design, UI work, backend schema, and ongoing tuning. That's a diverging system: it creates work that doesn't compound on its own.

The 20 habits already exist. Nobody has used them deeply enough yet to know what "leveling up" means to a real user. We'd be designing progression for people we haven't met.

"Pre-PMF needs convergence, otherwise the entire enterprise diffuses to nothing."

THE BET

Confidence that gamification solves a real problem right now: about 25%. No user has churned because the habit system wasn't gamified. No user has asked for collection mechanics.

Confidence it's the right thing to build in Q3 with real usage data: about 70%. Once we see how Teague and the men's circle actually use habits, the design writes itself.

WHAT WOULD CHANGE OUR MIND

  • Users explicitly ask for it
  • Habit dropoff at 2 weeks, clearly from boredom (not "life got busy")
  • Paul prototypes the UI and visual resonance is undeniable

For Paul

Open questions

Does the three-layer breakdown feel right?

Collection, then progression, then expansion. Or is there a different sequencing that makes more sense from a behavior change perspective?

What makes progression feel earned vs arbitrary?

Bronze/silver/gold tied to streaks + wearable outcomes. Is that the right signal? Or should tier-ups be more about coach-assessed readiness?

Is "collect habits" the right frame, or is it "build your program"?

Collecting implies breadth (try everything). Building implies depth (master a few). Our coaching methodology leans toward depth. Does the deck metaphor pull against that?

Would you prototype the visual?

If the card UI above resonates, a Figma prototype of the deck view in Kasane could be the thing that makes this undeniable. That's something you could do independently while we gather usage data.
