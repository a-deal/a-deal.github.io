Gap
Who writes the protocol?

You complete each other's blind spot. His practitioners write protocols. Your system helps patients execute them. Neither of you has to build the other's half.

Lab handshake

Malla integrates Rupa for lab ordering. Kiso interprets lab results and tracks trends. Practitioner orders labs through Malla, results flow to Kiso, coaching adapts automatically.

Parsley Health context

Parsley = the clinic. They employ the doctors. $199/mo. Raised ~$100M+ VC. Board-certified MDs with IFM training. Pivoting toward employer contracts (B2B2C). Malla advisory board member Tanya Mezher came from Parsley.

Malla = the operating system. They don't employ practitioners. They give independent functional medicine docs better tools. $89/mo per practitioner. Consumer membership at $65-75/mo connects patients to practitioners on the platform.

Kiso = the daily execution layer. Parsley proved demand for functional medicine at consumer prices but needed $100M+ to employ doctors. Malla's bet: you don't need to employ practitioners, just give them tools. Your bet: you don't need the practitioner for 80% of daily coaching. The AI handles follow-through between appointments.

Three layers: Malla (practitioner tools) -> Parsley-type practice (clinical care) -> Kiso (daily execution between visits).

Ask

Three questions to explore

The gap question

"What happens after a practitioner on Malla writes a protocol? How does the patient actually execute it between appointments?"

Opens the door to integration without pitching. If he says "that's a huge gap," you're in a real conversation.

The craft question

"You said healthcare software has been fluorescent lighting. What does the alternative look like for practitioner-patient communication?"

Bond on shared design philosophy. You're both building health tools that feel human.

The data question

"How do your practitioners think about wearable data and daily tracking? Is that coming into their workflow?"

Tests whether practitioners want the kind of data Kiso collects. If yes, that's the integration surface.
