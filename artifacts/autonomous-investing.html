The Core Insight
THE MODEL
DOESN'T
GENERATE ALPHA

Nof1.ai gave 7 frontier LLMs $10,000 each of real money to trade autonomously. No human intervention. Same market data, same execution access. The results were decisive.

Only one model (Grok) was consistently profitable. GPT-5.1 lost 12%. Claude 4.5 lost 51%. The models don't generate alpha. Purpose-built systems with proprietary data and integrated stacks do.

This is the same pattern as every platform shift. During the gold rush, the miners went broke. The people selling picks, shovels, and Levi's got rich.

The autonomous investing infrastructure layer is where value accrues: brokerages with APIs, portfolio management platforms, execution engines. Every agent trade flows through this infrastructure regardless of which model wins.

Falling knife

Regulatory Landscape
THE SEC IS
PERMISSIVE

The SEC withdrew its Predictive Data Analytics proposal in June 2025. No new rulemaking for AI-managed accounts is planned. The U.S. remains "technology neutral": no pre-approval of trading algorithms required.

This is the opposite of China, where regulators require full algorithm disclosure and approval before deployment. The U.S. approach: let the market develop, enforce existing rules after the fact.

This is bullish for the entire autonomous investing infrastructure thesis. No regulatory headwind for brokerages offering API access to AI agents, or for platforms managing autonomous portfolios.

Public.com launched consumer-facing AI trading agents on March 31, 2026. The fact that they could launch without special regulatory approval validates the permissive environment.
