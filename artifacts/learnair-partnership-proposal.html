The Evergreen curriculum

From Oversight
to Orchestration

Topical, not tool-based. Model-agnostic by design. Whether someone uses ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini is below the line. What we teach is above the line.

Topic 1

Military Planning Frameworks & Processes

Veterans already know this. They just don't know it transfers. Joint-service planning processes — MCPP (Marine Corps), MDMP (Army), NPP (Navy), JOPP (Joint) — and operating frameworks like OSMEAC, BAMCIS, TLP, COG, decentralized command, OODA Loop, and 9-Lines: all the same core skill. Defining boundaries, validating outcomes, driving results through ambiguity. These are the skills required to supervise AI systems. The curriculum surfaces this tacit knowledge and makes it explicit.

MAPS TO

Defining agent intent, setting success criteria, structured execution frameworks.

Topic 2

In-the-Loop vs. On-the-Loop

In the loop: human required for every step. You are the bottleneck. On the loop: human can correct, but the system moves autonomously. Order of magnitude efficiency gain. This is THE curriculum gradient. We teach the transition from tool use to system oversight.

MAPS TO

Delegation patterns, autonomy levels, when to intervene vs. when to let the system run.

Topic 3

Output Validation & Structural Honesty

AI systems select which facts to present. They can climb the wrong hill without telling you. Your team feels productive. They don't realize the oxygen is running out because nobody trained them to check. The supervision layer: knowing when the system is optimizing for the wrong goal.

MAPS TO

Verification frameworks, adversarial testing, distinguishing real progress from apparent progress.

Topic 4

Adversarial Collaboration

"Sir, respectfully, I think we're on the wrong hill." Veterans are trained to challenge assumptions up the chain. That's the skill AI oversight requires. Not agreement. Not blind trust. Constructive challenge that improves the output. This is what separates a tool user from an operator.

MAPS TO

Prompt refinement, red-teaming outputs, feedback loops that improve system quality over time.

Topic 5

Delegation & Feedback Architecture

How to specify what you want without over-constraining execution. How to design correction loops that catch errors early. How to structure feedback so the system gets better, not just compliant. Decentralized execution philosophy: define the outcome, trust the execution layer.

MAPS TO

Agent orchestration, multi-step workflows, human-in-the-loop design patterns.

Topic 6

Model Evaluation & Platform Agnosticism

We don't teach loyalty to a model. We teach evaluation as a skill. Which tool fits this task? How do you test that? What changes when the model changes, and what doesn't? The judgment layer is permanent. The tool layer is temporary. Graduates learn to navigate both.

MAPS TO

Tool selection, capability assessment, building workflows that survive model transitions.

Topic 7

Ecosystem Thinking

Operating in systems of systems. Inputs, outputs, dependencies, feedback loops. Understanding how a change in one agent affects the rest of the workflow. This is where military systems thinking, managing logistics, comms, and personnel simultaneously, maps directly to managing AI-augmented organizations.

MAPS TO

Workflow design, dependency management, operating across multiple AI tools and teams simultaneously.

Curriculum philosophy

Evergreen by design

Principles, not tools

Commander's intent. Feedback loops. Output validation. Managing ambiguity. These transfer regardless of which platform is dominant in 6 months.

Foundational, not just-in-time

Two types of knowledge: what you internalize (judgment, critical thinking, systems reasoning) and what you look up (API docs, market research). We teach the first kind.

The curriculum doesn't expire

The tools change. The judgment doesn't. LearnAIR can run cohort after cohort without rebuilding from scratch when the next model drops. A scaffold that can also be repurposed for non-veteran audiences.

The identity shift

The real outcome isn't the certificate. It's that veterans see themselves as capable of leading in AI-enabled ecosystems. Not AI literate. AI capable. That's the shift.
