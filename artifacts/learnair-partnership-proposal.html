LearnAIR: What to Say

Verbal talking points only. No document leaves your hands.

Step 1: Ask the Question First

Before presenting anything, get a number.

"Before we walk through what we've been working on, I want to make sure we're aligned on something practical. What can LearnAIR spend per month on this engagement?"

If they deflect or give a non-answer:

"Even a range is helpful. Are we talking $2K, $5K, $10K? I want to make sure what we've designed is realistic for where you are."

If they can't answer: Stop here. Don't present. Say: "That's okay. Why don't you and Teresa talk about it and get back to me? Once we have a number, I'll share the full scope."

Step 2: If They Give a Number

The shape of the engagement

  • 90-day engagement, 1099 contractor
  • $100/hr billed monthly against actual hours
  • Mike embeds in existing program, gets reps with real cohorts
  • Andrew designs curriculum architecture, front-loaded then fading
  • By day 90, a LearnAIR trainer delivers the new modules independently

The approach

  • Not building from scratch. Hooking into what you already have.
  • Evolving existing curriculum toward agent workforce skills
  • Veterans join as a sub-group in current cohorts first
  • Standalone veteran cohort is a day-45 decision based on real data

What we need from LearnAIR

  • An upcoming cohort within 30 days for Mike to embed in
  • Justin opening employer network for validation conversations
  • Teresa providing a concrete accreditation artifact by week 4

What success looks like at day 90

  • Mike running sessions independently
  • At least one new module piloted with a real cohort
  • 3+ employer conversations about graduate placement
  • Both sides know whether to continue, expand, or part ways

Do Not Share

Do not hand over any document. The following stay internal until terms are agreed:

  • The four-module curriculum design
  • The workstation toolkit concept
  • Competitive analysis or market research
  • Specific IP terms or ownership language
  • The full written proposal

These are leverage. They get shared after a spend number is confirmed and engagement terms are agreed.

If They Push for a Written Proposal

Say: "We have a detailed scope of work ready. Once we're aligned on budget and engagement terms, I'll send it over for review. I want to make sure we're building something that works for both sides before we put it on paper."
