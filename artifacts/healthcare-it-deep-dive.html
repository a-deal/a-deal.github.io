MUMPS

Massachusetts General Hospital, 1966. A PDP-7 with 4KB of RAM. Three developers fused an operating system, programming language, and database into one inseparable layer. It stored data as trees. Branches only existed when data existed. Fast writes, concurrent access, zero wasted memory.

The tradeoff: reading across patients requires walking every tree, one by one. MUMPS was built to answer "what is happening with this patient right now?" Not "what is happening across all patients."

Fee-for-service needs documentation. Value-based care needs measurement. The EHR was built to do the first, and the economy now runs on the second.

Under fee-for-service, providers are paid for each service. More tests, more visits, more revenue. Payers began experimenting with value-based care, where providers are paid for outcomes rather than volume. Medicare now ties a significant share of hospital payments to quality metrics and readmission rates.

This model requires tracking outcomes over time, comparing performance across providers, and identifying patients at risk before they deteriorate. All of these require reading across patients, the one thing the architecture can't do without risking the live clinical system.

Change Healthcare Breach / Feb 2024

No multi-factor authentication on a remote access portal. BlackCat ransomware encrypted systems responsible for 40% of U.S. medical claims. 192.7 million Americans exposed. UnitedHealth paid $22M in Bitcoin. Total financial impact exceeded $2.45 billion. 94% of hospitals reported financial damage.

The Pincer Strategy

Consumer Aggregation (OpenAI / ChatGPT Health, Jan 7 2026): Patients download and aggregate their own records, bypassing institutional barriers. When patients share their own data, HIPAA's institutional rules largely don't apply.

Enterprise Compression (Anthropic / Claude Healthcare, Jan 11 2026): Automate the $1T administrative layer from inside. Documentation, coding, prior auth, clinical trial management.

CMS Interoperability Framework

July 2025: 60+ companies (Amazon, Apple, Google, OpenAI, Anthropic) pledging data sharing criteria. The framework is voluntary. The binding deadline for FHIR-based data exchange is July 2026.
