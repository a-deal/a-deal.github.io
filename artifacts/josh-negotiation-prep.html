What this means for you: If Riverside exits while you're employed at SCRAM, your Ownership Works equity could pay out. This is potentially the most valuable part of your compensation that you don't yet understand. Don't negotiate salary until you know what this is worth.

Ownership Works: The Equity Program

What it is: A nonprofit founded in 2021 by Pete Stavros (KKR partner). It helps PE-backed companies create broad-based employee equity programs. 37 PE firms participate. As of late 2024: 113 companies, 163,000+ employees, $570M in equity payouts.

How it typically works:

  • Employees receive equity grants (not options) on top of existing wages and benefits
  • Typical payout: 6-12 months of salary when the PE firm sells the company
  • Payout triggered by a "liquidity event": PE sale, secondary buyout, or IPO
  • Vesting schedules vary. Common: 3-4 year vest with 1-year cliff
  • Some programs pay incrementally at performance milestones, not just at exit

Questions to ask Brad:

  1. What form does the equity take? (Phantom equity, profit interest units, RSUs?)
  2. What's the vesting schedule? Is there a cliff?
  3. What triggers a payout? Only a full sale, or are there interim distributions?
  4. Have any SCRAM employees received payouts yet?
  5. Is the grant sized relative to salary, role level, or tenure?
If the payout is 6-12 months of salary on a PE exit in the next 1-3 years, that's $50K-$100K on top of your base. That changes the math on whether $100K vs. $120K base even matters.
