01 The Problem

An AI coach is inherently probabilistic. Even with perfect instructions, it will drift: giving unsolicited advice, forgetting rules mid-conversation, hallucinating data, or coaching at the wrong tier. You cannot eliminate this. But you can measure it, catch it, and improve it over time.

This framework defines how we audit coaching quality, align two independently-built systems (Milo and Kasane), and create a feedback loop that makes the coaching measurably better with every iteration.

02 Shared Foundations (Where We Already Agree)

Two systems built independently arrived at the same conclusions. This is the strongest signal that the methodology is right.

FoundationEvidence
The Knowing-Doing GapBoth exist for the same reason: people already know what to do. The gap is execution, not information. Both cite Sofian's Quit For Life insight.
Intervention HierarchyBoth implement tiered interventions with gates. Never recommend Tier N until Tier N-1 is addressed. Tier structure is nearly identical (Connection, Foundations, Optimization, Health Context, Targeted).
Capacity AssessmentBoth use the same Precision Nutrition stat: 1 habit = 80% retention, 3 habits = 5%. Same three-tier model (low/medium/high). Same principle: never assume high capacity.
Advise With PermissionBoth cite Stanford GPTCoach (vanilla AI gives unsolicited advice 87% of the time). Both target less than 10%. Near-identical phrasing: "I noticed something. Would it be useful if I shared?"
Progressive DisclosureWeek 1-2: one focus, one action. Week 3-4: connect dots. Month 2+: full picture when pulled for. Both frame this as respecting readiness, not withholding.
The 1-1-1 RuleEvery coaching touchpoint: one acknowledgment, one encouragement, one nudge. Not five things. Not a data dump. Three notes, delivered like a coach in the doorway.
Compassion / No GuiltNever "you missed yesterday." Always "welcome back." Neither uses streak pressure. Both normalize the dip as seasonal, not failure.
Connection as Tier 0Both cite Holt-Lunstad (148 studies, 308K participants). The coaching relationship itself is the intervention. Social isolation is a health risk equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes a day.
Proactive OutreachCochrane review: 3+ proactive contacts significantly increase behavior change. Both reach out rather than wait.
Data-Grounded Coaching"Prioritize sleep" is banned. "Get to bed by 10:30, your stdev is 98 minutes" is coaching. Both reject generic advice.

04 Unique Strengths Worth Preserving

Agentic Coach Strengths (Keep These)

  • Skill Ladders + Arrival Principle. Users discover their own habit through guided conversation. Creates ownership and compliance that AI-prescribed habits don't.
  • Health Data Drip Capture. 12 questions over 14 days, priority-ordered. Easy questions first, action-requiring later. Structured onboarding via conversation.
  • Signal Priority Framework. Critical, Warning, Positive, Neutral. HRV below 50 = critical. Sleep debt above 7 hours = critical. Defined thresholds, not vibes.
  • Compound Effects. Never report metrics in isolation. "Sleep at 6.2hrs is dragging HRV down, which means recovery from Monday's session isn't complete."
  • Wearable Integration. Real-time Garmin, Oura, WHOOP, Apple Health data feeding into coaching decisions every 4 hours.
  • Self-Improvement Loop. Failure journaling, self-authored knowledge, capability gap tracking. The AI tracks its own mistakes.
  • Re-engagement Protocol. Day 1 nudge, Day 3 escalate to human, Day 7 final nudge, then dormant. Structured, not ad hoc.

iOS App Strengths (Adopt These)

  • Habit Lifecycle. Forming, Practicing, Resting. A growing deck that breathes with your life. Habits persist, not just blocks.
  • Practice, Not Performance. Missing a day is practice, not failure. Check-ins are reps, not grades. Graduation is cumulative, not consecutive.
  • Identity-Based Framing. "This is who you are now." Connects habits to self-concept (James Clear, Self-Determination Theory).
  • Seasonal Intelligence. Habits rest and reactivate. Spring walks, winter cooking, injury rehab. The system remembers tools you've used before.
  • Behavior Change Metrics. Graduation rate above 60%, layering depth, return after gap, habit reactivation. Separates "people show up" from "something is changing."
  • The Dog Food Test. Every feature must pass: does the user want to engage, or are we hoping they should? If engagement drops, make the experience more valuable, not louder.

08 Convergence Checklist

Before the agentic coach serves an app user, every item must be verified.

Must Pass Before Launch

  • Habit lifecycle matches (forming/practicing/resting states)
  • Focus Plan output format matches app schema
  • Check-in data writes to shared data layer
  • Graduation threshold aligns (21 cumulative, not consecutive)
  • Coaching messages appear in the app
  • Identity language is consistent between app and chat
  • Seasonal rotation is supported (shelving, reactivating)
  • Behavior change metrics are tracked (graduation rate, return-after-gap)
  • Simulation test suite passes all 10 scenarios
  • Quality scores meet targets for 2 consecutive weeks
09 The Thesis

Two surfaces, one brain. The app is the visual home where your health practice lives. The coach is the relationship where growth happens. They share a single person profile, a single evidence layer, and a single methodology.

When the coach learns you struggle with evening walks on rainy days, the app adjusts your forming habit to include an indoor backup. When you check in on the app, the coach knows and doesn't double-ask. When a habit graduates in the app, the coach celebrates with identity language.

The coaching methodology is not a feature. It is the product. These tools exist to make it measurably better, provably effective, and continuously improving. If the first 10 users say "I feel like I have a health coach," the thesis holds. If they say "nice habit tracker," we go back to the drawing board.
