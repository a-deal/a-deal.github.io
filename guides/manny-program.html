Your Program

Manny's 3-Day Strength Program

Full body, 3x a week. Simple progression. Built for your home gym. Your wife runs the same program at her own weight.

The Program

Two workouts, alternating. Train Monday, Wednesday, Friday. Add 5 lbs to each lift every session. When you stall, take 10% off and work back up. That's the whole system.

Week 1: A / B / A Week 2: B / A / B

Day A

Barbell Squat 3 x 5
Bench Press 3 x 5
Barbell Row 3 x 5

Day B

Barbell Squat 3 x 5
Overhead Press 3 x 5
Deadlift 1 x 5

The squat stays every session. It's the king of all lifts.

How to Get Lean and Muscular

You don't need to bulk and cut. Eat enough protein (aim for 150g+ a day) and keep lifting heavier. Your body recomps naturally when the training signal is strong and protein is high. Especially if you're not super lean yet, you can build muscle and lose fat at the same time.

From Dr. Mike Israetel

Keep 1-2 reps in reserve. Finish each set thinking "I could have done 1-2 more." Going to absolute failure every set burns you out and kills recovery. Save failure for the last set of the last exercise if you want to push it.

Control the stretch. On every rep, take 2-3 seconds on the way down. The muscle grows most in the stretched position. Don't bounce out of the bottom.

Want more size? Add one set of 8-12 reps at lighter weight after your 3x5 on bench and squat. That extra volume is where hypertrophy lives.

Warm-Up

5 minutes before every session. Non-negotiable.

Before You Lift

2 min Jump rope or jog in place
30 sec Arm circles each direction
1 min 10 bodyweight squats
1 min 10 hip hinges (touch toes, stand up)
1 min 1 warm-up set at 50% weight for your first lift

Cooldown

5 minutes after every session. This is where you stay injury-free.

After You Lift

90 sec Couch stretch (each leg)
60 sec Doorway chest stretch (each side)
60 sec Hang from pull-up bar or doorframe
30 sec 5 deep, slow breaths

For Your Wife

Same exact program. Same lifts, same progression, same warm-up and cooldown. Start with lighter weight and add 5 lbs per session at the same pace. The compound lifts work the same for everyone. She'll build functional strength and lean out without needing a separate "women's program." There is no such thing.

The Rules

Keep It Simple

Never miss two in a row. Miss a session, that's life. Miss two, you're building a habit of not showing up.

150g protein a day. This is the one nutrition thing that matters. Chicken, beef, eggs, protein shakes, whatever gets you there.

Add 5 lbs every session. This is the magic. Small, consistent progression is how you go from lifting light to lifting heavy without ever feeling overwhelmed.

Sleep matters more than supplements. 7+ hours. No supplement will outwork bad sleep.

Train together. You and your wife lifting together is the accountability system that actually works. Your kid sees parents who take care of themselves. That's the real program.
