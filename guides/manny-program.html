Back & Leg Recovery Notes

Given the September car accident and the back pain and left-leg numbness, here's how we adjust the program to keep building strength while protecting your spine.

Squat Adjustments

Wear a belt for every working set. This isn't a crutch. It increases intra-abdominal pressure and stabilizes your spine under load. Get a basic leather lifting belt if you don't have one.

Use a box or bench. Box squats control your depth and teach you to sit back, which takes pressure off your lower back. Sit back to the box, pause one second, then drive up. Same weight, same progression.

If numbness flares up mid-set, stop, drop weight 20%, try again. If it persists, switch to leg press for that session. Don't push through nerve symptoms.

Deadlift Adjustments

Swap conventional deadlift for dumbbell RDLs. Heavy dumbbells, 3x8. Hits your entire posterior chain without the spinal compression of a barbell off the floor. Same progression: add 5 lbs when all 3 sets feel controlled. When your back is feeling strong, you can work back to conventional deadlifts. Start light (50% of what you'd normally pull) and build from there. No rush.

See a PT first Numbness in the leg after an accident usually means a nerve is getting compressed. Imaging and a PT evaluation before loading heavier is worth it. Local picks in Concord: Golden State Ortho & Spine (spine specialty), Back On Track (2280 Diamond Blvd), Select Physical Therapy (in-network with most insurance).

Spinal Stability Warm-Up (Every Session)

Add these before your regular warm-up. The McGill Big 3: gold standard for back rehab.

1 min Bird Dogs (10 each side, 5 sec hold)
1 min McGill Curl-Ups (10 reps, hands under lower back)
1 min Side Planks (20 sec each side)

Getting Lean and Muscular

You don't need to bulk and cut. Eat enough protein (150g+ a day) and keep lifting heavier. Your body recomps naturally when the training signal is strong and protein is high. Especially if you're not super lean yet, you can build muscle and lose fat at the same time.

Three Technique Notes

Keep 1–2 reps in reserve. Finish each set thinking "I could have done one or two more." Going to failure every set burns you out. Save it for the last set if you want to push.

Control the lowering phase. Take 2–3 seconds on the way down. Muscle responds more to the stretch under load. Don't bounce out of the bottom.

Want more size? Add one set of 8–12 reps at lighter weight after your 3x5 on bench and squat. That extra volume is where hypertrophy lives.

Why Lifting Matters Especially for Caylie

What Caylie banks in the next few years is the reserve she draws against for the rest of her life. On bone density, fertility, and the role-model effect

Most conversations about women and strength training skip the "why." Here's my honest read on why I'm so pushy about Caylie lifting, not just "staying active."

Bone density is a one-shot window

Women hit peak bone mass around age 30. After that, it's slow decline, and it accelerates at menopause. Heavy compound lifts are the single most effective stimulus for building and maintaining bone density. No supplement, no cardio, no yoga does this the way squatting and deadlifting does.

Pregnancy prep and postpartum recovery

Stronger women have easier pregnancies and recover faster. Core and hip strength protect the back when the body's carrying extra load. Pelvic floor activation (trained directly by squats and dumbbell RDLs) matters a lot postpartum. I'd rather Caylie show up to a potential pregnancy with 6–12 months of strength work banked than not.

Metabolic resilience

Muscle is the body's biggest organ for insulin sensitivity. More muscle means better glucose handling, which correlates with better fertility, steadier energy, and slower aging. Cardio does not build this. Lifting does.

The role-model effect

Your son is 5. Whatever he sees Caylie doing with her body in the next 10 years becomes his model for "what women do." A mom who lifts isn't a small thing.

The short version Bone density, fertility, metabolic health, and what your son sees modeled every day. Four different arguments, one answer: barbell. Cardio and yoga are complements. They are not substitutes.

Sleep

The foundation. The phone-out-of-the-bedroom protocol, bedroom environment, and why this matters twice over for a family trying to conceive.

Sleep & Recovery

You mentioned both of you scroll social before bed and sleep quality is suffering. Based on what you told me, my bet is this is the biggest lever you've got right now, bigger than any supplement or protein tweak. Sleep is where the muscle you build in the garage actually gets built. It's also where testosterone is produced (for you) and where egg quality is protected (for Caylie). With you two trying for a second child, this matters twice over.

You can't out-train, out-eat, or out-supplement bad sleep. Rule II · The Foundation

The Phone-Out-Of-The-Bedroom Protocol

If you only do one thing from this page, do this one: phone charges in the kitchen, not on the nightstand. Buy a $20 alarm clock if you need one. That's it. That's the whole thing.

Blue light isn't the main issue. The real issue is stimulation. A 2-minute doomscroll spikes cortisol and dopamine right when your body needs to be winding down. You stop producing melatonin. You delay sleep by 30–60 minutes without realizing it.

The morning matters too. If your phone is next to you, the first thing your brain does is check it. That's a cortisol spike before your feet hit the floor. Phone in the kitchen means you start the day with 10 minutes of your own thoughts before the world loads in.

The experiment Two weeks. Phone in the kitchen, $20 alarm clock, lights out by 10:30pm. Report back on how you both feel. Happy to hear "didn't work" just as much as "game changer," both tell me something.

Wind-Down Routine (Start 60 min before bed)

T-60 Phone plugs in outside the bedroom. Lights dim.
T-45 Shower or bath. Warm water signals the body to drop core temp (which triggers sleep).
T-30 10-15 min with Caylie. Talk about the day. Or read a physical book.
T-10 Brush teeth, set room to 65-68°F, blackout mode.
T-0 Lights out. Same time every night, weekends included.

Bedroom Environment

Three variables. Get all three right once and your sleep improves every night for free.

Cool

Room temperature: 65-68°F.
Body needs to drop core temp to fall asleep. A warm room fights you.
AC, fan, or crack a window. Cheapest upgrade on this page.

Dark

Blackout curtains or sleep mask.
Any light (including the LED on your router) interrupts melatonin production.
Tape a piece of black electrical tape over every LED in the room.

Quiet (Or Consistent)

A white noise machine is $30 and worth every penny. It won't silence noise, it will mask it. Your brain stops tracking sudden sounds (a dog barking, a car door) and settles deeper. Especially useful if your son wakes up at night or you live near traffic. Ours is a Hatch Rest or a basic Homedics for $25 on Amazon.

Caffeine & Alcohol

Both disrupt sleep architecture even when you don't "feel" it. Two rules.

Caffeine, cut off by 2pm

Half-life is 6 hours. A 3pm coffee is still in your system at 9pm, keeping you in lighter sleep stages even if you fall asleep fine. If you want an afternoon pick-me-up, go for a 10-minute walk outside instead. Sunlight does what coffee does, without the cost.

Alcohol knocks you out, but destroys recovery

A drink makes you drowsy, but it crushes REM sleep, which is where the brain recovers and hormones get regulated. If you're going to drink, finish 3 hours before bed and hydrate. Don't use it as a sleep aid. It's the opposite.

Why This Matters for Fertility

You're trying for a second child. Sleep is the most underrated fertility lever for both of you.

For Manny

Testosterone is produced during deep sleep. One week of 5-hour sleep drops testosterone by 10–15%. That affects libido, recovery, and sperm quality. 7+ hours of quality sleep is the foundation of your T production.

For Caylie

Melatonin is a primary antioxidant in the ovaries. It protects egg quality. Phone light before bed suppresses melatonin. Getting her to 7–8 hours of dark, undisturbed sleep is one of the simplest, cheapest things you can do to support fertility.

Cheapest fertility move Neither of these costs a dollar. Phones in the kitchen, curtains drawn, room at 65–68°F. Two weeks. Then we talk labs.

Manny's Nutrition: Keep It Simple

Manny, you're already hitting 150g+ protein from week 1. You don't need a meal plan. You need one rule: 150g+ protein a day. That's the single thing that drives muscle growth and body recomp. Everything else is secondary.

The easiest way to hit it: front-load breakfast with 30-40g protein. That puts you on pace for the day without thinking about it.

The Math

Breakfast: 30-40g. Lunch: 40-50g. Dinner: 40-50g. Snack or shake: 20-30g. That's 150g+ without counting a single calorie. If you're short, add a protein shake after your workout.

For Caylie: Protein Target 100-120g

Scaled to her bodyweight. Same playbook: front-load breakfast with 30-40g, coast the rest of the day. Greek yogurt, eggs, chicken, Premier Protein shakes. If she wants to match Manny's 150g, nothing wrong with that.

For Caylie: Three Nutrients Worth Extra Attention

Folate (400-600 mcg/day): matters a lot for a healthy pregnancy. Leafy greens, lentils, fortified cereals, or a prenatal vitamin. Most OBs recommend starting a prenatal 3 months before trying to conceive.

Iron: women lose iron monthly. Red meat 2-3x per week, or pair plant sources (spinach, beans) with vitamin C to absorb better. Ferritin lab check if energy drops.

Vitamin D: affects hormone regulation and egg quality. 2,000-4,000 IU/day is a reasonable range unless she's getting a lot of sun. Both of you could probably stand to supplement.
