5. ELI5 glossary (the 12 terms to know)

  1. CRISPR — molecular "find and replace" for DNA. Cut, replace, or switch off specific sequences. Enables disease research, crop engineering, embryo editing.
  2. Genomics / DNA sequencing — reading the full instruction manual of an organism. Cost collapsed from $3B (2003) to $200 (2024). That's what made modern biotech possible. Next cost curve = synthesis (writing DNA).
  3. Synthetic biology ("synbio") — engineering biology like a circuit. Design DNA from scratch to make cells do new things. Endy is one of the field's founders.
  4. Bioeconomy / bioindustrial manufacturing — using living cells as factories. Yeast brewing insulin, bacteria making leather, engineered microbes producing jet fuel. Endy's thesis: could replace a meaningful fraction of petrochemicals.
  5. APIs (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients) — the actual drug molecule inside a pill. ~40% of global APIs come from China. Kuiken's central concern.
  6. Key Starting Materials (KSMs) — chemical precursors needed to make APIs. Even US-made APIs depend on Chinese KSMs. Kuiken's "dependency goes deeper than you think" argument.
  7. ARPA-H — health moonshot agency, created 2022, DARPA-modeled. Funds risky research too far-out for NIH. Palmer's "Responsible Innovation" role sits here. ~$1.5B/year.
  8. DARPA BTO — DARPA's bio office, created 2014. Military-relevant bio. Kerri Dugan used to run it. Invented some of the mRNA vaccine tech.
  9. NSCEB — Congressional commission (2022-2025). Final report April 2025. Chaired by Senator Todd Young. Palmer was staff designee to the chair. Probably the most-cited policy doc in the room.
  10. BIOSECURE Act — pending legislation to restrict federal contracts with Chinese biotech firms (BGI, WuXi, others). Kuiken supports.
  11. Biodefense / biosecurity — preventing biological threats. The 10:40 panel. Jaime Yassif ran NTI-Bio for years.
  12. "Misallocation" framework (Endy's) — NIH spends billions on biology applications and too little on biology tools. Your thesis mirrors this pattern in therapeutics. Lean on this term with Endy.
