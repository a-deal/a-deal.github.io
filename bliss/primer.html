01
The Thesis

The US regulatory-commercial system cannot produce definitive answers about non-patentable therapeutic compounds. Not approval. Not rejection. Silence.

Patent economics drive the outcome. At $2.6B per approval, only compounds pharma can monopolize get the trials. Everything else, even compounds with legitimate biology, lives in an evidence desert. Meanwhile millions of Americans self-administer via gray-market supply chains (Chinese synthesis labs, 80% dosage deviation from label, 8% bacterial endotoxin contamination). Population-scale uncontrolled experiment, zero data capture.

This is not a biology failure. It is not a biohacker failure. It is an evidence-production infrastructure failure. And it applies to any therapeutic compound the patent system will not evaluate, not just peptides.

North Star · Hold This All Day

You are pitching a compound-agnostic epidemiological layer for any therapeutic compound the patent system will not evaluate.

Peptides are the beachhead because Natasha's corpus is real, the gray market is dangerous, and Attia just made the category topical. Peptides are the first application, not the whole thesis.

The Hard Counters
Topol: "Show me the data."
Topol's demand is exactly right. The question is why the data doesn't exist. It's not because these compounds are inherently worthless. It's because the system structurally can't produce the evidence he's asking for. And his 20% impurity number actually undersells the problem. The largest independent testing dataset shows dosage deviating up to 80% from label. Topol is not an opponent. He is an unwitting ally.
"Attia says most peptides fail scrutiny. Why are you in this space?"
Attia is exactly right. That is why infrastructure to distinguish the narrow legitimate subset from the hype is the missing layer. Attia does it for one compound with a podcast. The question is how to do it for 200 compounds at population scale. That is infrastructure.
"RFK is the tailwind. You're riding a political wave."
The thesis predates the political moment and survives any political moment. Peptides are the beachhead. The infrastructure applies to any compound the patent system won't evaluate. That is durable across administrations.
"Why compound-agnostic rather than compound-by-compound trials?"
Compound-by-compound costs $2.6B per answer and produces one yes-or-no bit. The epidemiological layer produces continuous signal across hundreds of compounds at a fraction of the cost. It is the difference between one very expensive lab experiment and a field that runs for a decade.
"BPC-157 is snake oil."
Exactly. The system hasn't produced evidence either way. That is the gap.
"TB-4 had 17 trials and just failed Phase 3."
One compound with an answer. Dozens with zero. The infrastructure is for the zero-answer compounds. The frame is "the system should produce answers, even if the answer is no," not "more peptides will get approved."
"Isn't this biohacker wishful thinking?"
Millions are self-administering with 80% dosage variance from Chinese labs. Whether the compounds work or not, you have a population-scale uncontrolled experiment with zero data capture. That is a public health infrastructure problem, not a biohacker problem.
"What makes you qualified?"
I build health data infrastructure. 20 biomarkers scored against NHANES population data. I'm also on CJC-Ipamorelin with no structured way to know if it's working. That personal gap led me to a structural one.

Key Nuances You Need Cold

If a Stanford bioengineering PhD raises any of these, you cannot be flat-footed. Know the facts, have the response queued.

TB-4 / RegeneRx · Phase 3 just failed
J.J. Finkelstein spent 15 years on Thymosin Beta-4. 17 clinical trials. RGN-259 eye drops missed the SEER-3 endpoint. This is the strongest counter: "the system DID try, and it failed."
Response"That's one compound with one indication with an answer. There are dozens more where the system has not even produced a 'no.' And TB-4's failure actually supports my thesis: the frame is 'the system should produce answers,' not 'more peptides will get approved.' Do not claim evidence infrastructure leads to pharma developing analogs. Claim it produces answers, even when the answer is no."
Ipamorelin · Phase 2 failed on efficacy
Helsinn ran Ipamorelin through Phase 2. Safe but failed on efficacy. The system produced an answer. The answer was "no."
Response"The system produced an answer there. It was 'no.' That is what infrastructure looks like when it works. The gap is the hundreds of compounds where even 'no' was never produced."
BPC-157 · The single-lab problem
212 papers, 80% from Sikiric/Seiwerth at Zagreb. Cancelled Phase 1 in 2015, never published results. No identified receptor. BPC-157 is NOT "GLP-1 in the 1990s." GLP-1 had 2,158 papers, dozens of independent groups, and an identified receptor before pharma modified it.
Response"That's exactly the evidence gap. It's not 'promising compound needs trials.' It's 'the system hasn't produced independent validation OR refutation.' Just silence. Do not claim BPC-157 will work. Claim the system owes an answer."
Hudson Biotech · Phase 2 right now
Hudson Biotech is running Phase 2 trials on BPC-157, TB-500, and GHK-Cu currently. Someone is betting on clinical development. Mention this to show you know the landscape.
Use proactively"Hudson Biotech just started Phase 2 on three of these compounds. That is the first serious clinical bet. But there is no public evidence infrastructure capturing what is happening at population scale in parallel. Even a Hudson success would be one compound. The infrastructure is for the hundreds."
Finnrick methodology caveat
VC-backed (Naval Ravikant). Vendors pay for premium programs. Non-random sampling. Methodological limitations are real.
How to phrase"The largest independent testing dataset shows…" · NOT "research confirms…" · The numbers are the authority, not the source.
