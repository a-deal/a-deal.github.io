Kasane

重ね — to layer, to stack, to accumulate through repetition. The kind of word a grandmother would use. You kasane years of experience. You kasane effort, day after day, until something takes root.

What Kasane believes

Habits are layers, not tasks. Each one you build becomes part of who you are — not something you check off and forget. The product is patient: one habit at a time, evidence-based, coached by data you generate yourself. No 30-day challenges. No streak anxiety. No gamification theater.

The coach speaks at inflection points and stays quiet the rest of the time. Silence is the default. When the coach does speak, it's because the data says something worth saying.

Sentiment as signal layer (new — not yet in coaching engine)

Emoji + free-text over time becomes a sentiment signal that powers multiple coaching decisions:

  • Anchor habit detection: Consistently "great" ratings identify the habit that fits your life best. Protect it. This is the one to build layers around.
  • Struggle detection: Persistent "tough" ratings trigger coach intervention — rescope the habit, suggest group accountability, or bench it before it drags other habits down.
  • Lifestyle fit scoring: Sentiment + consistency together reveal whether a habit is sustainable identity-level or just grinding. A habit with perfect streaks but "meh" ratings is fragile.
  • Seasonal adaptation: As your health baseline rises and more habits graduate to practicing, sentiment reveals which layers still bring energy vs. which are coasting. Priorities shift by season — the data shows when.

Requires new field on CDCheckIn (reaction enum + optional note). Currently the model has: id, date, completed, note. The coaching engine (AIService) uses only behavioral signals today — this would be the first qualitative input fed to the AI prompt context.

Peakspan

Peakspan reframes the goal: not just avoiding disease (healthspan), but staying near your personal best. Kasane's layers are how you maintain peak across domains.

Longevity
How long do I live?
Binary. Not our lane.
Healthspan
How long do I avoid major disease?
Clinical markers, labs, diagnostics. Baseline's measurement layer (Ring 2).
Peakspan
How long do I stay near my personal best?
~90% of lifetime best in a specific domain. Kasane's behavior layer (Ring 1) — the habits that maintain peak.
The gap Kasane fills: You can pass every clinical marker while silently drifting from your personal best. A healthy A1C doesn't mean your VO2 max isn't declining. Normal bloodwork doesn't mean your sleep quality is where it was two years ago. Peakspan asks: are you still operating near your best? Kasane's layers are how you stay there.

What the mapping reveals

  • Measurement lives in Baseline, behavior lives in Kasane. VO2 max, RHR, strength benchmarks — Ring 2 metrics. The habits that maintain them — Ring 1. Peakspan is the reason you need both rings.

Sentiment becomes signal

Voice gives sentiment + context + triggers. Over time: anchor detection (consistently great), struggle routing (consistently tough), lifestyle fit (sentiment x consistency).

The sentiment signal layer
Anchor habit detection

Consistently "great" = this habit fits your life. Protect it. Build layers around it. The coach reinforces: "Walking reliably makes your day better — that's your anchor."

Struggle detection

Persistent "tough" (3+ in rolling window) = early warning before gaps appear. Coach intervenes: rescope, suggest group accountability, or bench before it drags other habits down.

Lifestyle fit scoring

High consistency + "great" = identity-level. High consistency + "meh" = fragile, grinding. Low consistency + "great" = loves it, can't stick it. Low consistency + "tough" = wrong habit.

Seasonal adaptation

As baseline rises and more habits graduate, sentiment reveals which layers still bring energy vs. which are coasting. Priorities shift by season — the data shows when.

Implementation note: Requires new reaction field on CDCheckIn (enum: tough/meh/good/great, nullable). The coaching engine (AIService) currently uses only behavioral signals — this would be the first qualitative input. Paul's voice check-in already captures note text ("felt amazing"), which is the raw material. The classification layer (AI-extracted sentiment from notes) is the bridge. See _andrew/sentiment-signal-layer.md for full spec.

Five-phase sandbox
Phase Days What happens
early0–2Too early for anything. Coach quiet. Just the habit and one small celebration.
exploring3–6Expand nudge can fire (streak >= 5 + all gaps == 0 + forming < 3). First chance to add a second habit.
calibrating7–13First weekly reflection fires. User sees their data reflected back for the first time.
deepening14–20Health context surfaces (Apple Health correlation). Midpoint reflection. The "oh damn" moment.
calibrated21+Sandbox complete. Journey summary: "started N, benched N, landed on N." Your plan is ready — but graduation is still 21+ days away.
Behavioral graduation
Criteria (all must be true)
  • Minimum 42 days (6 weeks) — Kaushal & Rhodes 2015
  • Zero 2+ consecutive gap episodes in rolling 60-day window
  • Currently clean (not gapping)
Design decisions
  • No visible countdown, no progress bar — arrives when earned
  • Fires as fullScreenCover from TodayView
  • Coach speaks: "[Habit] is part of who you are now."
  • Moves to Practicing, frees a Forming slot
  • No auto-suggest at graduation — next habit comes via nudge or weekly reflection
Key distinction: Day 21 is sandbox calibration ("your plan is ready"). Day 42+ is behavioral graduation ("this is who you are now"). The visual journey map makes this separation explicit.
Capacity guardrails
Expand gate

forming < 3 AND all gaps == 0 AND streak >= 5. All three must pass before the coach suggests adding a habit.

One-per-day rule

Suppresses expand nudge if any habit was created today. Prevents enthusiasm-driven overload.

Overload nudge

4+ forming + 2+ gapping = "bench one" surfaces instead of expand. Protects the user from themselves.

Load-aware AI

AI prompt receives load assessment. Light / Sweet spot / Moderate / Heavy. Won't encourage expansion if heavy.

Weekly reflection (3-step flow)
1
Evidence

Week summary with capacity + patterns. Per-habit coach cards with gap health labels (clean/cooling/gapping/resting). Rescope and bench actions for struggling habits.

2
Coach conversation

Phase-aware question, user free-text response, 12s loading animation, AI generates 2-3 sentence reply from actual habit data. Intent classified: expand / refine / hold.

3
What's next

Varies by intent. Expand: InterventionCatalog suggestions. Refine: rescoping first. Hold: affirming trailheads, soft "when you're ready." Day 21 adds journey summary + identity close.

Gap health model
clean
Showing up consistently
cooling
Early warning, 1 gap

Where gap health is used
  • SandboxNudge expand gate (all gaps must be 0 to suggest adding)
  • Weekly reflection evidence cards (each habit shows its gap health label)
  • AI prompt context (coach knows which habits are struggling)
  • Overload nudge trigger (2+ gapping = "bench one")
Layer discovery (InterventionCatalog)
Four foundation stacks

Recovery (6 interventions), Movement (6), Nutrition (6), Connect (6). Each with mechanism, expected outcome, time-to-effect, evidence tier, suggested anchor, key citation.

OutcomeRouter ranking

Maps 8 onboarding outcomes to layer weights (60% stored outcome + 40% gap fraction). Only suggests from layers where user has 1+ adopted habit (has momentum). Post-add preview shows next 1-2 unadopted interventions.

What we like
  • Behavioral graduation over calendar graduation. 42 days minimum, earned by consistency — not a countdown. This is the right call.
  • Capacity guardrails that interlock. Four mechanisms that protect the user from overloading. The overload nudge ("bench one") is especially good.
  • Gap health as a vocabulary. Clean/cooling/gapping/resting gives the coach and the user a shared language for habit status.
  • Weekly reflection structure. Evidence → conversation → action. Phase-aware questions. Intent classification (expand/refine/hold). This is where the coaching engine earns its keep.
  • Load-aware AI prompts. The AI sees the real state (light/sweet spot/moderate/heavy) and adjusts accordingly. No one-size-fits-all coaching.
  • Identity language at graduation. "This is part of who you are now" — not "congrats on your streak." The shift from doing to being.
Open questions
